About

ElectionLeaflets.org is an open archive of the literature published by political parties and candidates during UK election campaigns.

One-third of all spending during UK elections goes towards printing election leaflets. Leaflets are the most common way voters learn about their candidates.

This website ensures that this otherwise ephemeral literature is captured for journalists, analysts, and historians to use now and in the future.

If you have recently received any election leaflets through your door you can help by photographing or scanning them and uploading the images.

Referencing the site

If you are using this site in your publications, please ensure you provide a link back to the original image. Credit Democracy Club, ElectionLeaflets.org.

What is an election leaflet?

An election leaflet is a printed document sent to voters in an election. It can be sent by a candidate, political party, or other campaigning organisation. All election leaflets must include an imprint, as required by electoral law.

Anything uploaded to the site which does not meet the above criteria will be removed.
